Interval Method

  • Interval is a kind of Granulation method such that we only care about some range of prices instead of exact number.
  • If we treat prices as some random process, then the goal is to find the range which prices will hit with big probabilities.
  • Set loss floor and gain cap. We can think in this way: suppose the probability for gain is $p_1$ and loss is $p_2$; cap is $1 + r_1$ and floor is $1 - r_2$. Then $p_1 r_1 - p_2 r_2$ is our expect profit when assume principal is the same in every trade.
  • Intuitively, the greater $r_1$ is the smaller $p_1$ would be, same as $r_2$ and $p_2$. Assume there is some rough gain constant $g = p_1 r_1$ and loss constant $l = p_2 r_2$. Or put another way, we want to find $p_1, p_2, r_1, r_2$ such that $g > l$.
  • The key is how we find $p_1$ and $p_2$. One way is by "backtesting" such that by looking at similar day in history. The term "similar" may be defined by some kind of combinations of quantitative indexes.
