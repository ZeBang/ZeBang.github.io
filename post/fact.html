Math Fact and Tricks

Algebra

If $A$ and $B$ positive semi-definite, then $\text{trace}(AB) \le \text{trace}(A) \text{trace}(B)$.
Suppose $x,y\in \mathbb{R}^{n \times 1}$ are vectors. $A\in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}$ is a matrix. Then $\text{trace}(Ayx^{\prime}) = \text{trace}(x^{\prime}Ay) = x^{\prime}Ay$.
All norms are convex.
If $f(x)$ is convex and $\inf_{x \in \partial A}f(x) \le c$ then $\inf_{x \in \Omega/\partial A}f(x) \le c$.
If $\{ X_{n} \}$ is a stochastic sequence such that each element has finite variance, then $$X_n - \mathbb{E}(X_n) = O_p (\sqrt{\text{var}(X_n)})$$.
(Expectation and Variance of Quandratic Form) One can take the expectation of a quadratic form in the random vector $x$ as follows, where $\mu = \mathbb{E}(x)$, $$\mathbb{E}(x^{\prime} A x) = \mu^{\prime} A \mu + \text{trace}(A \text{Var}(x)).$$
